The High-Altitude Narrow Escape

HUALIEN, TAIWAN — Early on a Wednesday morning last April, a group of six experienced hikers were descending the rugged trails of Taroko National Park. They had planned this excursion for months, but by 7:58 AM, their "routine" trek turned into a life-altering incident. A massive 7.4-magnitude earthquake struck, causing a series of profound landslides that instantly blocked all exits from the mountain.

As the dust settled, the hikers realized the implications of their situation: they were trapped on a narrow, crumbling ridge 2,000 meters above sea level. While they were waiting for help, they shared their remaining water and tried to stay objective about their survival chances. For two days, local media coverage focused on the inevitable challenge of a high-altitude rescue. Before the specialized search-and-rescue helicopters finally arrived, the group had spent 48 hours exposed to freezing winds.

In a split-second encounter with a pilot, the hikers were hoisted to safety just as another aftershock rattled the cliff. While initial sensationalism in the news focused on their fear, the hikers' personal account revealed a different perspective: it wasn't just luck that saved them, but their calm justification for every survival decision they made.

3 · Write The Story Behind the Headline

Choose a simple, everyday incident from your life (a lost set of keys, a minor car problem, a missed bus…) and write about it as if it were a high-stakes breaking news story. Use objective language for the facts, but add nuance by explaining the background of the day before the event happened.

🎬 The three narrative tenses:
📅 Past Simple — the main actions: "I locked the door."
🎥 Past Continuous — the scene: "I was driving to work."
📖 Past Perfect — the backstory: "I had forgotten my keys."

💬 Try to use: inevitable, justification, repercussion, profound, encounter, implication.

  • 7–9 sentences
  • Past Simple for the main actions
  • Past Continuous for the scene-setting
  • Past Perfect for the backstory
  • At least 4 lesson vocabulary words
